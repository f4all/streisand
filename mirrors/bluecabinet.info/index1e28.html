Background

Glimmerglass Networks Inc. provides for cyber-security and lawful interception -- Optical Distribution for the Intellegence Community.

Glimmerglass is a privately held company financed by leading institutional investors.

" Glimmerglass Networks, Inc. develops and integrates optical cyber solutions. It offers intelligent optical systems; and intelligent peripheral systems, which include system control modules, lossless splitters, high-density splitters, and dual optical amplifiers... The company serves cyber security, intelligence and defense, lawful interception, cyber lab automation, and telecom network monitoring customers; and telecom central offices/POPs and submarine cable landing stations worldwide.

It offers its products directly; and through distributors and integrators in Europe, the Middle East, and the Asia Pacific " Businessweek


Products

NOTE: 6.3.2013: Glimmerglass Networks, Inc. has been issued patent application serial number 626812 "a patent application by the inventors Chau, Kelvin (Pleasanton, CA), filed on September 25, 2012, was made available online on May 9, 2013." [1]

" Glimmerglass Intelligent Optical System integrates powerful software with cutting-edge hardware to provide unmatched flexibility, performance, and cost savings for enabling Cyber Security and executing Lawful Interception on optical networks. Organizations use Glimmerglass to monitor and selectively intercept communications, distribute optical signals to one or many locations, and centrally manage the process on demand from remote locations. Furthermore, Glimmerglass leverages purely optical, photonic signal management to create a ‘transparent’ cross-connect. A transparent cross-connect accepts and reconfigures an optical signal without regard for its data rate or protocol. This means that a Glimmerglass IOS that manages 1G to 10G data rate signals today can manage 40G or 100G signals once the network evolves, without the need for hardware or even software upgrades." Glimmerglass

"The Glimmerglass Intelligent Optical System, a key component of the Glimmerglass CyberSweep™ solution, addresses these issues, allowing operators to rapidly and remotely access, monitor, and distribute optical signals for network monitoring and Lawful Intercept applications... CSPs, LEAs, and intelligence agencies use the Intelligent Optical System to rapidly access target optical signal streams and distribute them to signal processing and monitoring equipment, all without disrupting network traffic..." pdf

Board of Directors


Ret. Lt. Col. Robert Lundy: President & Chief Executive Officer

Robert joined the company in 2004 as the COO and was appointed President and CEO in May 2005. He is a member of the company Board of Directors.

Prior to joining Glimmerglass Networks, Robert was the co-founder and CEO of several early-stage ventures, including Opthos, Xtera and Wavtrace. Wavtrace, a leading supplier of millimeter wave broadband access systems, was acquired by Harris Corporation in 2000. Xtera operates today out of Dallas, Texas as the leading supplier of ultra-long haul optical transmission systems. Robert also held executive positions at IBM, including heading the first wireless business unit as Director and General Manager, and pioneering IBM’s PCRadio and data over cellular businesses. Prior, he led product line management at ROLM/IBM for the flagship IBM 9750. Robert’s first two years in industry were at Hewlett-Packard as a business development manager in the microwave and optical divisions.

Earlier, Robert served on active duty in the U.S. Army in command positions in tactical communications in the U.S. and South Korea, and in project officer positions in both R&D and the acquisition of Command, Control, and Communications systems. While in the private sector, Robert completed his career in the U.S. Army reserves with 20 years of service, retiring as a Lieutenant Colonel. He is a graduate of the U.S. Army Command and General Staff College.

Robert’s education includes a bachelor’s degree in both mathematics and electrical engineering concentrations from the U.S. Military Academy at West Point, a master’s degree in electrical engineering from Stanford University and a Masters in Business Administration from the Stanford University Graduate School of Business.

U.S. Army Lieutenant General (Retired) Michael Maples, Glimmerglass Strategic Advisory Board

"distinguished U.S. Army Lieutenant General (Retired) Michael Maples, former Director of the Defense Intelligence Agency, has joined its strategic advisory board, effective immediately. The strategic advisory board includes Frank Dzubeck, Michael Frankel, Tom Kelley and Martin Zimmerman.

“General Maples is one of the leading voices in defense intelligence, and his work for the U.S. Army and as Director of Defense Intelligence is unrivaled,” said Robert Lundy, CEO of Glimmerglass. “There are few individuals who understand intelligence requirements and opportunities better than General Maples. His strategic view further strengthens our advisory board as we continue to invest in and expand our defense, intelligence and law enforcement agency business. We are honored to have him on our board of advisors and look forward to ongoing collaboration.”

Lieutenant General (LTG) Maples served as the 16th Director of the Defense Intelligence Agency (DIA) from 2005 to 2009. In this role, he was the primary military intelligence advisor to the Secretary of Defense and the Joint Chiefs of Staff. During that time he also commanded the Joint Functional Component Command for Intelligence, Surveillance and Reconnaissance (JFCC-ISR) for the United States Strategic Command (USSTRATCOM). Before that, LTG General Maples served as the Vice Director of the Joint Staff.

“Glimmerglass breakthroughs in optical signal management technology are transforming intelligence gathering and distribution capabilities,” said General Maples. “I’m pleased to join the company’s advisory board and participate in the pioneering work Glimmerglass is doing to remotely and instantly enable the configuration of optical paths for mission critical entities and applications. Now that the world is ringed in fiber, and bandwidth requirements and capacity are growing daily, Glimmerglass optical innovations are central to the success of intelligence and defense initiatives.” Glimmerglass
