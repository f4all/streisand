Syriatel (Arabic: سيريتيل‎) is a privately owned telecommunications company founded in 2000.

Syriatel controls 55% of the local mobile telecommunication services market and has the largest subscriber base in Syria.

Its network covers 99% of the populated areas.

In 2008 Syriatel launched an Broadband Internet access service under the name "Surf".

Syriatel the service center 63 are distributed in all areas of Syria, and three call centers in both Damascus and Aleppo and Homs is to provide assistance to more than 25,000 subscribers each day by answering their questions.

About Us [7]

2 of Transworld's Board Of directors are from SyriaTel [8]

Emad Farid Mr. Farid joined the Orascom group in 1992 where he held different managerial positions. As of 2000, Mr. Farid joined OTH as the Chief Operation Officer, and was subsequently appointed as the CEO of Syriatel. Mr. Farid is currently the Group Chief Operation Officer at OTH. Moreover, Mr. Farid is a Board member & Chairman of the Board in several OTH subsidiaries.

Maan El Amine Mr. Maan El Amine joined OTH in 1999. Prior to his nomination as CEO of M-link Maan contributed to Hutchison stake's acquisition in 2005. Before that he was the CEO of Oasis, OTH subsidiary in DRC, since 2003, where he successfully managed the resurgence of the company and contributed to its divestiture. Since September 2001, he was marketing director of Mobinil, in the business development field within OTH. Prior to that Maan was the CTO and then CEO of SyriaTel trial network, after which the BOT contract was awarded to Syriatel. He contributed to the launch Mobinil in 1998, where he was the engineering manager. Maan started his career in 1994, with France Telecom Mobile Lebanon, in 1996 he was nominated system engineering manager. Maan holds a Maitrise in Electronics and Automation from Rennes I and a DEA from Paris VI Universities in France.

Board Members

  • Rami Makhlouf (Chairman)

is a wealthy Syrian businessman and the maternal cousin of President Bashar Assad.

He is the main owner of Syriatel and according to the Financial Times he is thought to control as much as 60% of the Syrian economy through his web of business interests that include telecommunications, oil and gas, construction, banking, airlines and retail. He is considered one of the most powerful men in Syria and according to Syrian analysts no foreign company can do business in Syria without his consent and partnership. He is a part of Bashar Assad's inner circle.
