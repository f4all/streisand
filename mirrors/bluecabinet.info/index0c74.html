Background

"Vislink plc is a global business, strategically focused on providing secure communication technologies to customers in our chosen markets.

We specialise in wireless, video and IP technologies together with the supporting management systems. Our products include the design and manufacture of microwave radio, satellite transmission and wireless cameras. We have manufacturing operations in the UK and the USA.

We have three international business units organized to serve our customers in Broadcast, Surveillance, and the related Services markets.

Urban Surveillance

Airborne Downlink

Airborne surveillance is an important contributor to the tactical picture. Real-time imagery provides a tactical advantage to crisis management professionals. Kamelyon, the latest High Definition digital video transmission technology from Vislink, provides a rugged and simple to use downlink solution with greater reliability and signal range than anything offered before. Kamelyon represents Vislink's Software Defined Surveillance network technology, forming an encrypted downlink system that is reliable and easily deployed. Vislink's unique technology forms a wide area cellular receiver network that simplifies airborne operations and provides secure distribution over IP to multiple agencies.

Unmanned Systems

Unmanned Systems provide mission critical visual imagery from aerial and ground based vehicles to deployed assets and command staff. Vislink's Kamelyon family of products provides real-time high quality video in either HD or SD from the sensor through to the distribution chain. Vislink's technology provides the end-to-end solution from the airborne transmitter and antenna to a complete ground control station (GCS) to support video collection, mission management, and command & control.

Board Members


John Hawkins Executive Chairman

Joined the Board in December 2010 as a non-executive director. He became Chief Executive on 1 April 2011 and was appointed Executive Chairman on 18 May 2011. He was formerly Chief Executive of Atex Media Command Limited and Chairman of Genus plc and prior to that was Chief Executive of Anite plc where he was responsible for taking the Group from a £68m loss to a £30m profit. He spent 25 years with Philips Electronics most recently as Global Head of Multimedia and was instrumental in formulating the DVD standard. John is also non-executive Chairman of Psion plc.


Ian Davies Group Finance Director & Company Secretary

Joined the Board in August 2012 as Group Finance Director and Company Secretary. He previously held the positions of Finance Director of Victoria PLC where he was appointed to the Board in March 2007. Prior to this, Ian spent 10 years in the aerospace sector, where he had become Financial and Commercial Director of Umeco plc's International Components Division. He is a chartered accountant and an engineering graduate.


Robin Howe BSc, FCIM Senior Independent Non-Executive Director

Joined the Board in 2006. He is Chairman of MetaSphere Ltd and Spinflow GmbH and is a director of Access2 Ltd and Waterscan Ltd. Previously he was CEO of UDEX Holdings Ltd and headed the Broadcast Systems Division of VITEC Group plc. Robin has built a career running successful technology businesses.


Oliver Ellingham BA, ACA Independent Non-Executive Director

Joined the Board in October 2007. He is a former Head of Corporate Finance (Europe) at BNP Paribas and senior executive within BNP Paribas UK. He also previously held senior management roles within Charterhouse Bank and Robert Fleming. Oliver previously served as a nonexecutive director with McCarthy & Stone plc, Notting Hill Housing Trust and Cenkos Securities plc. He currently holds non-executive directorships in a number of companies including the Irish Bank Resolution Corporation Limited and the Naafi Pension Fund. Oliver is also Chairman and owner of Woking Storage Solutions.


John Varney BA Independent Non-Executive Director

Joined the Board in October 2011. He has spent 35 years in the broadcast industry, including Director of Technology and Chief Technology Officer for Granada. He was Global Chief Technology Officer at the BBC, and has spent the last five years working as an advisor and NED for emerging technology companies, combined with work across a broad range of organisations inside and outside the broadcast sector. John is a member of OFCOM's Advisory Committee for England.


Andrew Sleigh MA, FInstP, CPhys Independent Non-Executive Director

Joined the Board on 1 October 2011. He has worked primarily within the Ministry of Defence and was Group Managing Director of the Defence and Technology sector at QinetiQ plc, going on to become the Group Technology Officer. Andrew holds several advisory positions, including Vice Chair to the European Advisory Group for security research and between 1998 and 2009 he was a member of the OFCOM Spectrum Management Advisory Board. Andrew is an Adjunct Professor at Imperial College Business School.

Mike Payne Chief Executive, Vislink Inc.

As MD of Vislink Inc. Mike is responsible and accountable for our offices and people in Billerica and Vista, USA, and heads up Vislink's Surveillance business. Prior to his appointment as MD, Mike held the position of Vislink Group CTO, responsible for the coordination of Vislink's overall product and technology strategy. During his CTO tenure, he played a key role in helping to position the group to take advantage and begin to drive efficiency via its core technology developments.

Prior to Mike's two year assignment as Group CTO, he had had over 20 additional years of experience in the microwave communications industry. Positions held during his tenure at MRC included VP of Marketing & Business Development, VP of Operations, VP of Engineering and Product Line Manager.

In his marketing and business development roles Mike and his team were responsible for MRC's product roadmap which included launching MRC's developing IP communications strategy, which will help provide the base for next generation News Gathering and Law Enforcement products for the group.

Prior to his marketing role, Mike was responsible for MRC's Operations where his five year tenure began with Vislink's acquisition of MRC.

Mike earned his BSET and a BSEE from Wentworth Institute of Technology and an MBA from Suffolk University both located in Boston Massachusetts. He holds a US Patent for: Methods and Apparatus for Transmitting Analogue and Digital Information Signals. A patent which represents and protected the critical technology used in Microwave Radio Communication's long-haul product portfolio, leading to over 80% of market share for the US DTV transition.


Stephen Rudd Chief Executive, Vislink International

Stephen joined Vislink in September 2004 as Managing Director of its satellite systems division, Advent Communications, and was appointed MD Vislink International in May 2011, being responsible and accountable for our offices and people in Hemel Hempstead, Dubai, Singapore.

Prior to joining Vislink Stephen spent six months with Pixology plc, a leading provider of innovative software and services in the digital photographic industry, overseeing the roll out of major retail digital photo networks in the USA and UK.

From 1993 to 2004 Stephen held several senior roles within the Datacard Corporation, a world leader in secure I.D. and credit card personalisation systems, latterly responsible for subsidiary operations in the UK, France, Germany and Spain. Stephen created strong groups spanning geographies and culture positioning Datacard as a major supplier of solutions in the financial and intelligent identification markets. Stephen was also involved in several acquisitions and mergers during his tenure with Datacard.

Prior to that Stephen was Business Unit Director for Spectra-Physics corporation for six years, a leading supplier of laser based data capture systems for the point-of-sale and stock applications.

Stephen spent five years with Copal Systems, the Japanese manufacturer of photographic processing systems, introducing the "one hour" photo print service into the UK high street.

Stephen started his career with an Olivetti subsidiary as a key account manager training new recruits.
