Background

Pinion Software Inc was previously known as "INFRAWORKS" and is a subsidiary of GigaTrust / GigaMedia Access Corporation.

1.31.2008: "GigaTrust, a developer of digital rights-management applications, announced Tuesday that it has purchased the assets of Pinion Software, an Austin-based publisher of DRM utilities for computer-aided design applications. Terms of the deal, which closed Jan. 31, were not reported. As a result of the transaction, GigaTrust said it could now offer its clients in the enterprise-level manufacturing, aerospace and defense sectors a rights-management solution for every facet of their businesses. Pinion is a privately held company headquartered in Austin, Texas. Based in Herndon, Va., GigaTrust maintains regional offices throughout the United States." [1]

" Pinion Software offers software products, solutions, and services outside of North America through distributors.

" Pinion Software, Inc. provides information security solutions in the emerging technical rights management marketplace. Its products protect intellectual property during design collaboration, outsourced contract manufacturing, and other technical business processes.

Pinion Software, Inc. was founded in 1998 and is based in Austin, Texas. As of January 31, 2008, Pinion Software, Inc. operates as a subsidiary of GigaMedia Access Corporation. " Businessweek

Products

"The company’s products include;

  • Sharesafe Desktop, a security application that protects the information; and pinion receiver, a reader-type application for secure content.

It offers solutions, such as secure engineering and technical data, secure financial and investment information, control partner and vendor communications, protect executive communications, and secure content for remote/offline distribution. The company integrates an embeddable file-security packaging engine with corporate directory adapters, and offers packaged directory adaptors to support the integration of its file security products with existing corporate directories and systems.

It also offers a variety of products to meet the file security needs of PTC users and provides solutions to enable existing applications, systems, and business processes. The company was formerly known as Infraworks Corporation. " Businessweek

  • Darryl Worsham, Chief Executive Officer, President and Director

Mr. Darryl Worsham served as Vice President of Business Development at GigaMedia Access Corporation (alternate name: GigaTrust) since February 2008. Mr. Worsham joined GigaTrust in February 2008. Mr. Worsham served as Vice President of Worldwide Sales and Marketing of Pinion Software Inc., since 2001. He served as Head of Sales/Marketing of Pinion Software Inc. Mr. Worsham joined Infraworks from HotData, a leader in one-to-one marketing solutions, with over 14 years of sales, marketing and management experience. Mr. Worsham served as Vice President of Sales and Marketing at HotData where he was responsible from a small business market to a targeted vertical enterprise focused company. Mr. Worsham was instrumental in building the sales, marketing, professional services and business development teams from the ground up to deliver the new HotData 1-to-1 service offering to fortune 500 companies. Mr. Worsham served as Vice President and General Manager of the Americas region for Micrografx. Mr. Worsham was responsible for organizing the region into profitable customer units. He has held several key positions during his 11 plus year tenure including General Manager of Sales and Marketing for the Global Corporate Sales Division, Enterprise Account Management and Channel Marketing. Mr. Worsham handled duties in product management and spearheaded the development of the Internet Products Group. Micrografx went public in 1991. He served as Director of Pinion Software Inc. Mr. Worsham holds a B.S. in Education from Oklahoma State University.

  • George Friedman Ph.D., Cornell University

George Friedman,Ph.D., Founder of Pinion Software, Inc(formerly known as Infraworks Corporation. ) and served as its Chief Technology Officer. Dr. Friedman is an internationally recognized expert in security issues relating to Information Warfare and computer security. He has over 25 years of experience in programming, database construction, computer project management, international research, and intelligence methodologies and analysis. Dr. Friedman has briefed widely on security and national defense matters, including senior commanders in all armed services, the Office of Net Assessments, SHAPE Technical Center, the U.S. Army War College, National Defense University, The National High Performance Computing and Communications Council, Oak Ridge National Laboratory on the Objective Force Warrior concept, and U.S. Space Command. He has developed a patented file security technology and a suite of products that protect digital property. Dr. Friedman helped pioneer military modeling technology and in 1994 established the Center for Geopolitical Studies at Louisiana State University. He serves as Director of Pinion Software, Inc. Dr. Friedman is also a well-known author of books and articles on international affairs and business intelligence, including The Future of War and The Intelligence Edge. Dr. Friedman has been a featured speaker at many government and commercial events covering topics such as Information Technology in the 21st Century, Digital Rights Management, and Intelligence and Security for the 21st Century. He has lectured widely on defense and national security issues and has appeared on numerous television and radio shows including National Public Radio, Lou Dobbs Moneyline, CNN’s American Morning and ABC’s This Week as an expert on computer security, information warfare, and international affairs. Dr. Friedman received a B.A. from the City College of New York and an M.A. and Ph.D. from Cornell University.
