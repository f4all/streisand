Link Balancer - Announced in September 2008, to optimize and aggregate internet connections from different providers.

Backup services - In November 2008, the company announced a service to back up data in the cloud, including on-site backup with data deduplication and off-site data replication for disaster recovery.

In January 2009, Barracuda added message-level backup for Microsoft Exchange and Novell GroupWise, integrating Barracuda Backup Service with Yosemite Backup, formerly Tapeware.

Purewire Web Security Service - In October 2009, in conjunction with its acquisition of Purewire, Barracuda Networks launched the Purewire Web Security Service which is a software as a service offering for Web filtering, content security, and safe web surfing.

NG Firewall - In February 2010, Barracuda announced its NG Firewalls to protect enterprise network infrastructures. The firewalls integrate web and email filtering, intrusion prevention, layer 7 application profiling, and network access control into one platform that is centrally managed across multiple distributed enterprise network locations. NG Firewalls are available both as hardware or as a virtual appliance and include wide area network traffic optimization.

Products

    • Network Web Filters - various models with photos
    • Barracuda Web Filter-410 with photo
  • "The Barracuda Web Filter is a complete content filtering and application blocking solution designed to enforce acceptable Internet usage policies by blocking access to objectionable content and unauthorized Internet applications. Through the ability to easily block or allow Internet applications, administrators have the power to better secure the network perimeter, limit bandwidth usage and eliminate unwanted communications...

For most Internet applications, the Barracuda Web Filter can control applications efficiently by profiling the ports and destination IP addresses used by those applications. However, peer-to-peer applications such as Skype communicate with other peers at varied IP addresses rather than centralized servers, and they utilize a number of fallback ports that include commonly used ports for other protocols, including HTTP and HTTPS. To be able to perform application blocking on applications such as Skype, the Barracuda Web Filter utilizes real-time packet inspection technology to analyze protocols independent of their port or destination email server. By integrating the protocol analysis with policy controls, the Barracuda Web Filter enables control of peer-to-peer applications based on time range, user, group, or subnet. Board Members

  • Dean Drako (CEO)

Dean Drako serves as the Chief Executive Officer and President of 3SP Ltd. (a subsidiary of Barracuda Networks Inc.). Mr. Drako serves as Advisor of PacketTrap Networks, Inc. Mr. Drako is responsible for overall corporate strategic direction and product development. He serves as Chief Executive Officer and President of Barracuda Networks, Inc. Mr. Drako serves as Vice President of Product Engineering at the 3DO Company. Prior to founding Barracuda Networks in 2002, he served as President, Chief Executive Officer and Executive Vice President of Velosel Corporation. He founded Velosel Corporation and closed two rounds of venture financing, oversaw product development and was responsible for the strategic direction. Prior to Velosel, Mr. Drako founded Boldfish. He founded Design Acceleration, Inc., and served as its President and Chief Executive Officer. Mr. Drako served as an entrepreneur in residence for SoftBank. He was instrumental in the development of the PowerPC architecture with Apple Computer. He has been Chairman of the Supervisory Board at Barracuda Networks AG since December 3, 2009. Mr. Drako holds a B.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor and M.S. in Electrical Engineering from the University of California, Berkeley.


  • Michael Perone (CMO)

Michael Perone serves as an Executive Vice President and Chief Marketing Officer of 3SP Ltd., a subsidiary of Barracuda Networks, Inc. Mr. Perone Co-founded Barracuda Networks, Inc. in 2002 and serves as its Executive Vice President and Chief Marketing Officer. Mr. Perone serves as an Advisor to Kettera Corporation, Peerflix. Mr. Perone served as the Vice President of Sales and Marketing at Barracuda Networks, Inc. Mr. Perone was responsible for overall marketing and communications strategy, including corporate brand management and identity. He has long been a visionary in delivering free Internet access to users worldwide. Mr. Perone started his career as a systems engineer and spent several years working in corporate IT at both GE Aerospace and NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratories. In 1998, he Co-founded Address.com, a leading free email and Internet access service with over 2.5 million users. Prior to Address.com, he co-founded Spinway, Inc. He also co-founded Affinity Path, the fastest growing private label ISP in the world, powering and supporting over 5,000 ISPs throughout North America. He serves on the Board of Directors for Address.com. Mr. Perone has been a Member of the Supervisory Board of Barracuda Networks AG (Formerly, Phion AG) since December 3, 2009.


  • Zach Levow (CTO)

Zachary Levow serves as Chief Technology Officer of 3SP Ltd. Mr. Levow Co Founded Barracuda Networks, Inc. in 2002 and serves as its Vice President of Engineering. Mr. Levow serves as Chief Technology Officer of Barracuda Networks, Inc. Mr. Levow leads the continued development of all Barracuda Networks' solutions. He is a pioneer in mail server development, remote access, and the free Internet space. Prior to Barracuda Networks, he was co-founder and Vice President of Engineering for Affinity Path, a self-service, private label and profitable DSL/dial-up ISP with over 4,000 partners. Prior to Affinity Path, he was co-founder and Chief Technology Officer of Spinway, Inc., the fastest growing ISP in history, reaching eight million customers in just eight months before being acquired by Kmart Corporation in November 2001. During his tenure at Spinway, he was the lead developer of Spinway architecture, one of the world's largest client server applications. He has also worked as a software development and architectural software design consultant for clients such as Sun Microsystems, Computer Sciences Corporation, Cadence Design Systems, and Motorola, Inc. Mr. Levow holds a BS in Mathematics and Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon University.


  • David Faugno (CFO)

David Faugno serves as the Chief Financial Officer of Barracuda Networks, Inc. and its subsidiary 3SP Ltd.

Mr. Faugno served as the Chief Financial Officer of Actona Technologies, Inc. He has global financial and business operations experience across a number of technology sectors. He served as the Director of corporate finance, mergers and acquisitions of Cisco Systems, Inc. Prior to that, Mr. Faugno served as the Vice President for Operations of Actona Technologies Inc., a leading wide area storage vendor that was acquired by Cisco. He served as the Chief Financial Officer of Soltima, Inc. In his eight years with AT&T, he held a variety of financial, sales and operations roles, including Chief Financial Officer of the Europe, Middle East and Africa region, in which he restructured AT&T's portfolio of investments and joint ventures in the region. He has been Vice Chairman of Supervisory Board at Barracuda Networks AG (Formerly, Phion AG) since December 3, 2009. Mr. Faugno holds a B.S. in accounting from Rutgers University and an Executive MBA from Duke University.
