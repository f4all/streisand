People Responsible

Owners, Investors, Partners, Board Members, Major Shareholders

Shraga Katz, Chairman

Present: Shraga Katz serves on the board of directors of Siverge Networks and Green SQL . He is also a board member of Rhythmia Medical . Katz has more than 30 years of experience in the technology sector, 20 years of which have focused on the communications industry.Katz is a two-time winner of the Israel Defense Award, Israel’s most prestigious recognition for research and development. Katz is a Venture Partner at Magma Venture Partners which specializes in early-stage investments in communication, semiconductors, internet and media.


1996 - 2005: Founder, President and CEO of Ceragon Networks Ltd . (NASDAQ: CRNT), a global provider of high capacity wireless networking solutions for mobile and fixed operators and private networks.

Prior to founding Ceragon, Katz served in the Israeli Defense Forces for 17 years where he held a number of positions including leading the Electronic Research and Development Department of Israel’s Ministry of Defense .


Education: a B.Sc. from the Technion, Israel’s Institute of Technology and an M.B.A. from Tel Aviv University.

Rami Hadar, Director, President & Chief Executive Officer (2006 - present)

Hadar joined Allot in order to complete the company's transformation to a service provider-focused global business. For more than 20 years, he has been establishing and leading telecommunications companies and developing global businesses that address the needs of fixed and mobile service providers. Early in his career Hadar founded and served as CEO of CTP Systems (micro cellular networks) until its acquisition by DSP Communications (NASDAQ: DSPC). Hadar continued with DSPC's executive management team for two years, and subsequently the company was acquired by Intel. Hadar went on to co-found Ensemble Communications, a pioneer in the broadband wireless space and the WiMax standard, where he served as executive vice president, sales and marketing. Following that, Hadar served as CEO of Native Networks where he was instrumental in successfully shaping the company into a market-driven provider of MPLS based solutions to tier-1 telecoms and in orchestrating the company’s ultimate acquisition by Alcatel. Hadar holds a B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from the Technion, Israel’s Institute of Technology.

Yigal Jacoby, Director

Yigal Jacoby co-founded Allot Communications in 1996 and served as both the Chief Executive Officer and Chairman of the Board of Directors until 2006, when he retired as CEO. Jacoby continued as Chariman of the Board until November 2008. In 1992, Jacoby founded Armon Networking, a manufacturer of RMON-based network management solutions, which was sold to Bay Networks in 1996. Following this acquisition, Jacoby continued on as General Manager of Bay Network’s Network Management Division from 1996-1997. Prior to this, he held a variety of engineering and marketing management positions at Tekelec, a manufacturer of telecommunication monitoring and diagnostic equipment. Throughout his career, Jacoby has founded several start-ups in the communications field, serving on their board of directors and taking an active role in guiding the companies. Jacoby has a B.A. cum laude in Computer Science from the Technion, Israel’s Institute of Technology and an M.Sc. in Computer Science from University of Southern California.

Nurit Benjamini, Director

Nurit Benjamini has served as a board director of Allot since 2007, bringing over 15 years of experience to the position. Benjamini currently serves as the CFO of Wixpress Ltd., a company that provides online tools that enable anyone to create web flash content. Prior to joining Wixpress, Benjamini served as CFO of CopperGate Communications Ltd., a leading fabless semiconductor company in home entertainment networking between 2007 and 2011. Between 2000 and 2007, Benjamini served as CFO of Compugen Ltd. (NASDAQ: CGEN), where she managed a private placement and the company's IPO in August of 2000. Earlier in her career Benjamini served as CFO at Phone-Or Ltd. and as CFO at Aladdin Knowledge Systems Ltd.. Nurit holds a B.A. in Economics and Business, and an MBA in Finance, both from Bar Ilan University, Israel.

Steven D. Levy, Director (2007 - present)

Levy currently sits on the board of directors and the audit committees of two other technology companies, publicly held PCTel (NASDAQ: PCTI) a broadband wireless solutions provider, and privately held GENBAND Corporation , a leading developer of next-generation media, session and security gateway solutions.

Until February 2010: Director of the publicly held Zhone Technologies (NASDAQ: ZHNE).

2005 - 2007: Director of the publicly held Tut Systems from October 2005 until its acquisition by Motorola in March 2007.

1998 - September 2005: Managing Director and Global Head of Communications Technology Research at Lehman Brothers. prior to 1998: Levy held a number of management positions on Wall Street, including Director of Telecommunications Research at Salomon Brothers, Managing Director and Head of the Communications Research Team at Oppenheimer & Co. and a senior communications analyst at Hambrecht & Quist.

Education: a B.Sc. in Materials Engineering and an MBA from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute

Itsik Danziger, Director

Itsik Danziger has over thirty five years of experience in senior executive positions in the telecommunication technology industry and about ten years of experience as a board member in nonprofit organizations. Currently, Mr. Danziger serves as an active chairman of Galil Software, a software development outsourcing company. He serves as an active board member in several other technology companies.

From 1985 through to 2007, Mr. Danziger has been with the Comverse Technology group in various senior management positions including: President of Comverse Network Systems, where he led the company from a startup to a world market leader of messaging solutions with annual revenues of over $1B; President of Comverse Technology Group and active Chairman of the Comverse subsidiary – Starhome.

Prior to Comverse, Mr. Danziger worked in various R&D and management positions in Tadiran Telecom division, later acquired by ECI.

In the NGOs sector, Mr. Danziger currently serves as an active board member of Avney Rosha, the Israel institute for school leadership, IVN (Israel Venture Network) and several other organizations. He was a member of the Israeli National Task Force for the Advancement of Education (Dovrat Committee), and was chairman of two of its subcommittees.

Mr. Danziger holds B.Sc cum laude and M.Sc in Electrical Engineering from the Technion and M.A cum laude in Philosophy and Digital Culture from Tel Aviv University.
