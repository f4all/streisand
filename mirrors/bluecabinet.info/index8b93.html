Background

Calls itself a Non-Profit Organization

CTC Affiliates

"Concurrent Technologies Corporation was formerly known as Metalworking Technology Inc. and changed its name to Concurrent Technologies Corporation in 1992.

The company was founded in 1987 and is based in Johnstown, Pennsylvania with an additional office in Stafford, Virginia. It has locations in Alabama, Connecticut, Florida, Georgia, Indiana, Maryland, Michigan, Missouri, New Jersey, North Carolina, Ohio, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, Virginia, and Washington.

Concurrent Technologies Corporation, a scientific research and development services company, provides management and technology-based solutions to federal and state government agencies, and the private sector in the United States.

It undertakes applied scientific research and development activities on behalf of its clients; and provides solutions for quality improvement, effectiveness enhancement, and rapid technology transition and deployment.

It offers acquisition support; decision support; design and development; information management; knowledge management; modeling and simulation; program and project management; rapid prototyping; research, development, testing, and evaluation; studies and analysis; software engineering; spiral development; systems engineering and integration; technology transition; and training and education services.

It also operates network of testing laboratories that provides biometrics, chemical analysis, coatings testing, corrosion testing, failure analysis, mechanical and physical property testing, metallography, nondestructive evaluation, and precision measurement services.

It undertakes programs in the areas of advanced materials, manufacturing, and engineering; environment and sustainability; healthcare solutions; information technology solutions; intelligence and cyber solutions; learning and human performance; logistics and readiness; power and energy; and safety and occupational health. Board Members

  • Edward Sheehan Jr., Vice Chairman, Chief Executive Officer and President

Mr. Edward J. Sheehan Jr., has been President of Concurrent Technologies Corporation (CTC) since July 2008 and also has been its Chief Executive Officer since January 3, 2009. Mr. Sheehan also serves as the President of the CTC Foundation. He served as Chief Financial Officer and Senior Vice President of CTC and was responsible for managing CTC's assets, financial requirements, financing relationships and policy development. Mr. Sheehan joined CTC in 1993. Mr. Sheehan possesses over 29 years of corporate, management, and programmatic experience associated with a variety of assignments for large and small businesses located throughout the United States. He participated in the start up and operation of several manufacturing and professional service firms. He serves as Chairman of CTC Foundation, and Vice Chairman of Concurrent Technologies Corporation. Mr. Sheehan serves as a Director of Pennsylvania Chamber of Business and Industry, Concurrent Technologies Corporation and Catalyst Connection.

He serves as Member of advisory board of University of Pittsburgh at Johnstown. Mr. Sheehan earned his degree from the University of Virginia.

  • Daniel DeVos, AmeriServ Financial, Inc.

Mr. Daniel R. DeVos, Dan served as the Chief Executive Officer of Concurrent Technologies Corporation from October 1988 to January 2, 2009 and its President from October 1988 to July 2008. In 1994, CTC was "spun off" from the University of Pittsburgh Trust, its original parent, and established as an independent, nonprofit, scientific research and development organization. He has been a Director of Ameriserv Financial, Inc. since 1991. He serves as a Director of Concurrent Technologies Corporation.
