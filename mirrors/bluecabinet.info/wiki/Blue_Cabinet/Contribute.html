How To Contribute to the Blue Cabinet Wiki Project

Qualifications

edit 'Do-acracy'

Telecomix is a decentralized cluster of jellyfish hacktivists and activists for Information Freedom, Anti-censorship, Freedom of Communication. Telecomix projects and operations are a Do-acracy where basically each person decides what they are interested in or where their skills and experience may be helpful.

To participate in the Blue Cabinet Wiki and join the Telecomix IRC #bluecabinet channel, you do not need to be a hacker or a computer expert.

The only requirements are;

  • Datalove
  • Be Brilliant - Kind and helpful to other "Cabinistas" in the #bluecabinet IRC channel.
  • Learning how to use the Wiki to add data.
  • Online research
  • Reading comprehension
  • A do-acracy requires more than showing up and lurking in the #bluecabinet IRC channel (HINT):p

It is okay to lurk for a few days if you are shy. However, Cabinistas are friendly. Best way of participating is to read about the Blue Cabinet project, a couple of the Wiki pages and then announce in the #bluecabinet chan "Hi, I am here to help. Is there anything specific that I can do?"

  • No doxxing of Cabinistas or creating drama. Anyone Trolling or Flooding the #bluecabinet channel will be kicked, repeat offenders banned.

How The Wiki Works

edit 'Wiki101'

How to add data to the wiki?

  • Go to the main page of the Blue Cabinet Wiki
  • Log In and Register your name/any name or your nick. After you log in you will see more tabs -- Edit, History etc.
  • Click on a company name and then "Edit" then add your data and click "Save" at the bottom.
  • Repeat each time you have more data to add.
  • If you don't log in, you cannot add data.

Sometimes the wiki will time out and log you out. This can be a pain in the ass if you had been adding data. Don't panic. Back arrow to the previous page that you had added data on and copy the entire page. Log in again, go to the company page you were on, erase the data and paste your data and SAVE. Once your data has been Saved, you can use "History" to find it again. Therefore, it is recommended that you Save your data entries frequently.

How to make a link

  1. To make an external link, put the URL in brackets, for example [http://www.telecomix.org Telecomix] will be Telecomix after saving.
  2. To make an internal link, within Blue cabinet, use double brackets. See this section for details.

Do this if you would rather learn from an example: Look at this page in the "edit" tab on top, then scroll to the bottom. Save the page. Look at both views of a company listed, to see how it is made :)

How to indent (make a space before a word)

Use colons for line indents. Colon looks like this :
For multiple line indents, just use more colons. For example, if you enter this,

Sentence
:First indented sentence
::Second indented sentence

you'll see this after saving,
Sentence

First indented sentence
Second indented sentence
  • Use the # sign for a numbered list.
  • Use an * asterisk for a bullet-point list.

What about photos?

The wiki does not display photos, pictures or other images. Please just include the photo URL within brackets, e.g. [hxxp://blahblah.com/blah.png] Photos should be included for the wiki sections, "Products" and "People Responsible."

What if you find a surveillance company that is not yet listed?

Please, MAKE SURE YOU HAVE A URL for the company to be referenced. A URL reference is required BEFORE adding any NEW company to the wiki.
Reason - There may be multiple companies or products with the same name. It is important to uniquely identify a new entry in the wiki by its URL.

  1. Log in
  2. Go to "List of Companies" and "Edit"
  3. Find where the company will fit in alphabetically.
  4. Add company name and enclose it in double brackets, like this [[Blue cabinet/new company]]
  5. Then "Save"
  6. Click on the [[ ]] link you just made (it is a wikilink). It will take you to a blank page.

What To Look For

edit 'Checklist'

Separate facts and suspicions carefully

"Someone told me" (Hearsay) is not good enough for the Blue Cabinet Wiki. All data entered on a wiki page must be supported by a company url or supporting data via emails, signed/stamped documents with pdfs published online.

How to find the surveillance vendors

Use a search engine to find the company website, if the website link is not yet listed, please add it.

Once you find the company website, look for data that corresponds to the topics listed in the Blue Cabinet wiki page i.e. Background (usually found under "About Us"), Products, Tech Partners, Board of Directors (usually under Corporate Governance or Management).

Criteria

Look for all the product "solutions" or abilities listed below. There maybe one or more, please try to find and list as many as possible.

Most company websites will include their own "Search." That is one way of discovering. Another is to do a web search using the "company name + criteria" i.e. "Ericsson Lawful Intercept". Please only list the Products that can be used in surveillance. If it is Product that does not match any criteria listed below, please do NOT include it on the wiki page.

Please remember to include the url source of the data that you are entering on a wiki page.


What to look for to add to a wiki page

  • Sells technology to authoritarian and/or non-democratic states.
  • Markets products to authoritarian and/or non-democratic states.
  • Partnered with other companies listed in the Blue cabinet.
  • Knowingly sales to dictatorships/totalitarian states (i.e., evidences exists).
  • Deep Packet Inspection (DPI)
  • Deep Content Instection (DCI)
  • Lawful Interception/Interception (LI) - includes tapping of phones
  • Filtering
  • Traffic redirect/modifies Packets in real time.
  • Data Mining / Collection
  • Data Retention
  • Monitoring
  • Data Analysis
  • Tracking - geo-location of end point users
  • Reports to Law Enforcement Agencies(LEA) and/or Govt Agencies
  • Advertises surveillance technology as surveillance tech (i.e., terrible animations of police in server rooms watching people through RDP applications).
  • Develops software or chips with specific surveillance purposes.
  • Persona Management
  • Defends the selling of surveillance tech by claiming it is the operators responsibility.
  • Blocking/Censorship of websites (Blacklist/Whitelist)
  • Provides integration to specifically assist in data capture, analysis, and data retention centers
  • How many countries does the company sell products in?
  • Has company made extraordinary efforts to improve their image as an ethical technological institution, without changing their practices (i.e. PR campaigns, essays, news letters).


Bonus points to note

  • Has actively criticized human rights violations, while not hypocritical.
  • Has withdrawn sales / services when a country violates human rights.
  • Has adapted or changed technological setups to promote democratic movements.
  • Has actively participated in policy making to promote freedom of speech / human rights.
  • Has taken extra measures to stop their products from being sold to authoritarian and non-democratic countries.

Recommended sources for finding data

Useful sites include BusinessWeek, Forbes, TechCrunch to find names and bios/CV on the Board Members.

News stories -- Bloomberg, Forbes and the Wall Street Journal have been covering surveillance companies. News stories should go under "Reports"

Bugged Planet, Project PM and WikiLeaks - The Spy Files and Syria Files have data on surveillance companies. So does Wikipedia. You can cross-reference their data and place under "Reports", please include their URL as the source if you are copying.

Can you copy/paste the data you find?

Yes, as long as you include the url link and name of source when quoting.

Align to the far left when you paste the data = Backspace all the way to the left or else it might end up with some funky looking box around your data entry once you have saved it.

It is HIGHLY recommended that you do your own research rather than solely someones website or wikipedia page. Their data can be outdated or incorrect. Also, their source links may no longer be functioning. Therefore, please double-check your copy/pasta before dumping :)

If there is extensive information in the basic research, consider creating a new and separate page for it. If you are not sure how to do this, please ask for guidance in the #bluecabinet IRC channel.
