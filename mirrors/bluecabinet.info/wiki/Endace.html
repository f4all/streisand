Background

The Endace story

Our commercial genesis in 2001 was with the manufacture of the first high-speed DAG® I/O cards. A family of DAG cards capable of capturing 100% of network traffic from the full range of network interfaces – Ethernet (10/100/1000), 10GigE, SONET/SDH, PDH/TDM, and Infiniband options – was subsequently developed.

By 2005 our DAG cards were in use in major telcos, government agencies, and financial institutions around the globe. Through working with our Tier 1 customers, we identified the need for a purpose-built capture system based on our proven DAG technology, and we started developing our EndaceProbe™ and EndaceSensor™ families which were launched in 2007.

Powered by our next-generation network monitoring and recording architecture, our families of EndaceProbe and EndaceSensor systems offer a wide range of configurations suitable for monitoring network segments from the edge to the core of a network, and supporting every network type.

Thanks to the power of the underlying Endace Platform, which combines an open, Linux-based operating system – OSm – with a sophisticated software stack, Endace Systems are capable of monitoring multiple network segments simultaneously, delivering 100% packet capture, nanosecond accurate timestamping, and providing a single, authoritative source of captured network traffic to any application that requires it for monitoring, measurement, analysis, security, forensics, and other network functions.

The underlying OS and software stack of the Endace Platform enables Endace Systems to be connected together in a centrally deployed and managed ‘fabric’ providing the power to see all across an entire network – even on complex networks that span the globe. Each system is capable of running multiple network monitoring and recording applications – including in-house custom applications, open-source applications, and best-of-breed commercial applications from a wide selection of Endace Partners.

In addition, Endace Systems come with EndaceVision –delivering immediate out-of-the-box value by providing solutions to a wide range of common network requirements, such as IDS, network analysis and forensics, packet forwarding, and more.

People Responsible

Mike Riley Chief Executive Officer

Mike joined Endace in November 2006 as VP Worldwide Marketing before taking up the role of CEO in July 07. Mike has over 25 years' experience in the networking and security industry. Prior to joining Endace he served for four years as Vice President of Marketing and Strategy at Network Engines, Inc., a leading manufacturer of server appliances for the security and storage networking market. In this role he led Network Engines' Marketing and Corporate Development initiatives, including the launch of the Network Engines NS-series of security appliances in partnership with Microsoft Corporation. Before Network Engines, Mike served as Chief Marketing Officer at Sonexis, Inc., and Senior Vice President Worldwide Sales and Marketing at Artel Video Systems, Inc.

He holds a Bachelor of Science Honours Degree in Electronic Engineering from the University of Southampton, UK.


Stuart Wilson Chief Technology Officer

Stuart joined Endace Technology in December 2005 as VP Engineering before taking up the role of CTO in 2007. As CTO, Stuart is responsible for defining Endace's strategic technology plan. Stuart has more than 20 years' experience in a variety of senior engineering roles. Immediately prior to joining Endace, Stuart worked at Philips Radio Systems in the UK, as well as spending time at Hewlett-Packard, and Agilent Technologies. During his career Stuart has helped to bring many successful products to market, including high-speed systolic arrays for space-based image processing, custom ASICs, and market-leading SONET and PDH test equipment. As CTO he spends much of his time with customers in overseas markets.


Spencer Greene Senior VP Product Management and Marketing

Spencer joined Endace in October 2011 from Juniper Networks to head up Product Management and Marketing. He has more than 20 years’ experience in the computer networking industry and is widely regarded as both an expert in his field and a visionary.

Spencer was originally the founder of Layer 5 networks which was sold to Juniper Networks in 1999. Between 1999 and 2011 he held a range of senior posts within Juniper including VP Product Management, VP Corporate Development and VP Junos OOPS. He is widely quoted in the industry, has a significant number of technology patents to his name, and is based in Silicon Valley, California.


Neil Livingston Chief Product Officer

Neil Livingston joined Endace in September 2009 and has responsibility for product development and engineering. Prior to joining Endace Neil held the role of COO at ProvencoCadmus, a New Zealand company specializing in EFTPOS solutions. Neil has extensive experience in both the wireline and wireless telecommunications sector, having held the role of General Manager at Modempac, a private telecommunications specialist, and Ericsson NZ, where he was instrumental in managing and growing business with both Telecom NZ and Vodafone NZ. Before coming to New Zealand he worked in Sweden for Ericsson.

Neil holds a Global Management Diploma from Thunderbird University, Arizona, USA, and a mini-MBA from Auckland University, New Zealand.


Chris Komatas VP Operations

Chris brings more than 15 years of international product management and marketing experience in the telecommunications industry to Endace. In his career, Chris has worked for Ericsson in both NZ and the UK as well as spending time at Juniper Networks in the USA. Chris has held both corporate and field marketing leadership roles, with responsibility for the creation and launch of new network solutions across service provider and enterprise customers. He has a broad knowledge of the service provider and enterprise markets, business models and network and security technologies as well as in-depth knowledge of wireline, wireless, and content/data center architectures. At Endace Chris is responsible for Product Lifecycle Management and Operations for all Endace Products and Services worldwide.

Chris has a Bachelor of Technology (Honours) in Manufacturing and Industrial from Massey University.


Michael Kelley VP Global Services

Michael joined Endace in July 2010 as VP Global Services, bringing nearly 20 years' experience in various roles spanning Support Engineer to Head of Professional Services. Most recently, Michael was responsible for the creation of Orange Business Services' Wireless LAN practice, which led to him being appointed the Wireless LAN Practice Director. Prior to Orange Business Services, Michael held the position of Head of Professional Services Americas and was a member of the original management team for Radianz, which has become the de facto means of interconnecting clients for electronic trading. Previously Michael held various roles at Equant and SITA.

Michael holds a Bachelor of Aviation Management (Magna Cum Laude) degree from Auburn University's Samuel Ginn College of Engineering and College of Business.


Kate Parsons Chief Financial Officer Kate joined Endace in October 2009 as Financial Controller, responsible for the day-to-day management of the Group's finance function. Having graduated with a Bachelor of Commerce degree from the University of Auckland, Kate commenced her training as a chartered accountant with Coopers & Lybrand in Auckland, and was admitted to the New Zealand Institute of Chartered Accountants in 1998. Kate worked abroad in Dubai, Canada, and the UK for seven years, gaining exposure to a variety of industry segments including banking, oil and gas, and business consultancy. Since returning to New Zealand, Kate has held Group Finance Manager roles, most recently at Framecad Solutions Ltd, an exporter of building technology solutions for cold formed steel construction. Kate has been responsible for the overall financial management of the Endace Group since January 2011 and was appointed Chief Financial Officer in June 2011.


Jason Moore Senior VP of Sales, USA

Jason joined Endace in June 2007 to grow the appliance sales within the EMEA region, increasing revenue in both financial services and European government before becoming General Manager Sales EMEA in April 2010. In July 2011 he moved to the US to lead sales for the Americas.

Prior to joining Endace, Jason was the Sales Director for Northern Europe at iDirect Technologies, a US-based satellite communications firm that grew from a startup to become the market leader. Prior to joining iDirect Jason was the Director of Sales for Western Europe and Government Services at EuropeStar Ltd, a startup satellite operator co-funded by then Alcatel Space and Loral Space. EuropeStar provided global connectivity solutions to international government organizations for secure communications, as well as national broadcasting and telecommunications agencies.

Jason was educated in radio communications at the Army Apprentices College, Harrogate, prior to completing eight years of service in the Armed Forces (Royal Signals).


Neil Templeman VP of Sales, APAC

Neil joined Endace in April 2010, first as the Account Manager and then the Director for the APAC region, before becoming Vice President, APAC Sales in April 2011. Originally from the UK, Neil brings more than 15 years' sales and management experience across the networking and security market. Prior to joining Endace Neil spent a number of years with the Dimension Data group both in the UK and Asia in a variety of senior positions. In the UK Neil was responsible for growing Dimension Data’s new business across the Northern region and driving its voice and data convergence business into the top tier accounts.

Prior to Dimension Data Neil worked at Anite Networks, which became Cable &Wireless. At Cable & Wireless Neil led the sales of their data and hosting portfolio, which soon became one of the fastest growing service lines.

At Endace Neil is responsible for leading the growth and profitability of the Asia Pacific region including Japan.


Paul Snow VP of Sales, EMEA

Paul joined Endace in May 2010 as an Account Director for the EMEA region before becoming Vice President, EMEA Sales.

Paul brings more than 15 years’ experience in various roles from Sales, Project, to Program Account management. He began his early career as an aerospace software engineer with Smiths Industries, subsequently becoming an Account Manager at EDS and HP, and has had responsibility for growing a number of consultancy businesses in the UK Secure Government and Defence arenas.

Paul is responsible for leading Endace’s growth and profitability in the EMEA region. He holds a Bachelor of Science (Honours) degree in Computing and Business Management.
