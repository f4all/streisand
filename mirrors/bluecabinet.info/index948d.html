Background

"Bull SA operates in the information technology market. The company assists large enterprises and government agencies to transform their information systems by applying its know-how in three fundamental areas: the supply and integration of enterprise information systems, the development of application software, and the operation of secure information systems.

Business The company’s operations focus on two related businesses: Infrastructure products and related support activities, such as supercomputers and large servers, data storage solutions, administration and security software, through products developed and manufactured in-house or by partners; and IT services which range from consulting to systems integration and outsourcing. They both target IT and Operations departments of large and medium-sized organizations." BusinessWeek

Tech & Business Partners

Partners

The company has a business relationship with Intel in the area of microprocessors[1] .

Bull also works with IBM on its Escala servers[2]

and with NEC for its entry-level range of Intel-powered servers (marketed as the NovaScale Universal)[3].

The company also works with players in the fields of data storage (Blue cabinet/SERPIKOM EMC[4], Symantec-Veritas[5], NetApp[6], and VMWare[7] )

and software (Business Objects, Informatica, Microsoft, Oracle, Red Hat, SAP, SAS, and Infor).

Bull is a participant in the main Open Source communities, including the Linux Foundation, OW2, QualiPSo, JBoss, or Apache.

Competition

The company’s competitors are IBM, HP, Sun, and Fujitsu-Siemens.

SGI and Cray are competitors in supercomputers.

In services, Bull faces competition with Cap Gemini, and Atos Origin." BusinessWeek
