Background

It was formerly known as Globetel Communications Corp. and changed its name to Sanswire Corp. in 2008. Further, the company changed its name to World Surveillance Group Inc. on April 19, 2011.

Symbol: WSGI (OTCQB)

Issue Type: Common Stock

Sector: Aviation/Defense

State/Country Incorporated: Delaware, USA

Years In Business: 6

Years Public: 6


"World Surveillance Group Inc. designs, develops, markets, and sells autonomous lighter-than-air (LTA) and unmanned aerial vehicles (UAV) worldwide. Its products would carry payloads that provide security and/or wireless communications from air to ground solutions at various altitudes.

The company’s airships, when integrated with electronics systems and other technology payloads, are designed for use by government-related and commercial entities that require real-time intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance or communications support for military, homeland defense, border control, drug interdiction, natural disaster relief, and maritime missions.

Its business focuses primarily on the design and development of UAVs that provide situational awareness and other communications capabilities through the integration of wireless capabilities and customer payloads. The company unveiled its mid-altitude LTA UAV, the Argus One, which represents a new airship design and is equipped with its stabilization system that autonomously controls the level of rigidity of the airship in flight and an integrated payload bay capable of initially carrying approximately 30 pounds of high technology sensors, cameras or electronics packages. Argus One is an unmanned autonomous airship with automated control for individual body modules for improved flight stability and aerodynamic control. The Argus One can wirelessly transmit critical live video and other information generated by its payload of electro-optical or infrared sensors, cameras or other high technology electronics directly to a ground control station or system.

The company also focuses on the development of a mid-altitude airship that features a semi-rigid, cigar based, non-modulating design which it calls the SkySat. The SkySat airship is a traditional airship design and is designed to carry heavier payloads for longer durations. It also offers the Stratellite, which is a high altitude airship. Businessweek

Tech & Business Partners

Technological partnership with Eastcor Engineering and a technological relationship with L-3 Communications (C2S2 Division), a prime contractor in command, control and communications, intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance, government services, aircraft modernization, and maintenance and electronic systems.

It has a relationship with Global Telesat Corp. (GTC), a provider of satellite-based asset tracking and monitoring services for governments and commercial users.
