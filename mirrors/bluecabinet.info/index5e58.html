Background

  • As of September 15, 2006, RSA Security LLC operates as a subsidiary of EMC Corporation, following the $2.1 BILLION acquisition. [1], includes RSA Security Inc. [2]

" RSA Security LLC and its subsidiaries provide solution to protect online identities and digital assets primarily in the United States. The company’s solutions help organizations protect their information and manage the identities of the people and applications accessing and exchanging that information.

It offers technology solutions, including authentication and credential management, access management, identity administration, and data protection.

In addition, the company provides business solutions, including regulatory compliance, password management, consumer identity protection, portal and partner integration, mobile workforce security, and credit/debit card information protection.

It serves banking, insurance, technology, hardware, software companies, professional services, manufacturing, distribution, transportation, telecommunications, government [NSA], healthcare, and pharmaceuticals companies.

The company sells and licenses its products and services directly to end users through Sales Force, a network of distributors, original equipment manufacturers, and value-added resellers.

"RSA Security Inc. provides internet security solutions. The Company offers products for electronic security such as authentication, encryption, and public key infrastructure..." [3]

Arthur "Art" Coviello — Executive Chairman, RSA

The Security Division of EMC [7]

"Mr. Arthur W. Coviello, Jr., also known as Art, has been an Executive Vice President of EMC Corporation since September 2006. Mr. Coviello served as the President of RSA Security LLC, a Security Division of EMC from March 1999 to February 10, 2011 and served as its Chief Executive Officer since January 2000.

He has strengthened RSA's position in the e-security market through the successful acquisitions of Xcert International, 3G International, TransIndigo and Securant Technologies, and through strategic partnerships with Microsoft and Accenture.

He has more than 30 years of strategic, operating and financial management experience in high technology companies. He served as the Chief Executive Officer of RSA since January 2000 and also served as its Acting Chief Financial Officer. He served as an Executive Vice President of RSA from September 1995 to March 1999, Chief Financial Officer and Treasurer from October 1995 to August 1997 and Chief Operating Officer from January 1997 to March 1999. He joined RSA in 1995 and was a driving force in its rapid growth.

He served as the Chief Operating Officer and Chief Financial Officer of CrossComm Corporation from March 1992 to January 1994.

He serves as a Co-Chair of TechNet New England. He was a leading member of TechNet's Chief Executive Officer Cyber Security Task Force. He has also held various finance and management positions in graphics software and semiconductor equipment companies.

He serves as a Co-Chair of National Cyber Security Summit's Corporate Governance Task Force, a public-private initiative co-organized by U.S. Department of Homeland Security and leading industry associations.

He serves as an Executive Chairman of RSA Security, Inc.

He is a founding Board Member of Cyber Security Industry Alliance (CSIA).

He has been a Director of AtHoc, Inc. since June 2012.

He has been a Director of EnerNOC, Inc. since June 2008 and serves as its Lead Independent Director.

He served as a Director of Sana Security Inc.

He served as a Director of RSA Security Inc. since 1999.

He served as a Director and Lead Independent Director of Crossbeam Systems Inc.

He has spoken at numerous conferences and forums around the world.

He holds a Bachelor of Science in Accounting from the University of Massachusetts." Businessweek


Joseph B. Lassiter, III

"Prof. Joseph B. Lassiter, III, Joe serves as Faculty Co-Chair of the Harvard Innovation Lab at Harvard University. Prof. Lassiter is a Co-Faculty Advisor to the HBS Student Business Plan Contest.

He served as the President of Wildfire Communications Inc., from July 1994 to February 1996.

Prof. Lassiter served as Vice President of Teradyne Inc., from January 1974 to February 1994. He joined Teradyne in 1974 as a Product Manager while on sabbatical from MIT. As a general manager, he was responsible for organizations ranging from start-ups to international, multi-plant businesses. His research focuses on high potential ventures including both those formed as new companies and those formed within existing organizations.

He began his career at MIT's Department of Ocean Engineering as an Instructor in 1970 and was promoted to Assistant Professor in 1972. He developed and taught a course on marine mineral resource economics. He lectured in hydrodynamics, marine transportation, and computer simulation modeling. In a joint program with Harvard Law School, he lectured on marine legal / regulatory policy. His research focused on forecasting economic and environmental consequences of offshore oil and gas development. He was appointed to the MIT-led National Academy of Engineering study on the future of engineering education.

He joined Harvard University Graduate School of Business Administration in September 1996, where he is MBA Class of 1954 Professor of Management Practice. Prof. Lassiter teaches Entrepreneurial Marketing in the MBA Program and Marketing Strategy in the Executive Education Program.

He has been an Independent Director of RSA Security LLC since 1996.

He serves as a Director of Bluefin Robotics Corp. and 1366 Technologies, Inc.

He was elected to Sigma Xi.

He is active in new ventures and serves on the boards of the Cambridge Chapter of the Center for the Quality of Management.

He received his BS, MS, and PhD from MIT and was awarded National Science Foundation, K.S. Adams, Jr. and Eugene and Margaret McDermott Fellowships." Businessweek
