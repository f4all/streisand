Background

Presently (5.2.13), using the name "LEA - AID" (LEA-AID Acquisition) for the company. Also receives GSA license "Contract Holder GS-07F-0048H" Current through Modification PO-0067- Effective February 25, 2013 Contract Period: August 1, 1998 through July 31, 2013

Data Universal Number System (DUNS) number: 078485714

  • March 19, 2013: SEC Revokes Registration Of Registered Securities Of Law Enforcement Associates

The registration of the registered securities of Law Enforcement Associates Corp. has been revoked. The company had repeatedly failed to file required annual and quarterly reports with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC or the Commission). Thus, the company violated a crucial provision of the federal securities laws that requires public corporations to publicly disclose current, accurate financial information so that investors may make informed decisions. The revocations were ordered in an administrative proceeding before an administrative law judge.

"On March 8, 2012, the voluntary petition of Law Enforcement Associates Corporation for reorganization under Chapter 11 was converted to Chapter 7. It had filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy on July 27, 2011."


"Law Enforcement Associates Corporation manufactures and markets surveillance and intelligence gathering products, and vehicle inspection equipment worldwide.

It provides audio intelligence gathering equipment, including radio frequency disguised transmitters, receivers, and repeaters for use by law enforcement agencies.

It also offers video surveillance products comprising disguised miniature cameras, video transmissions, and recording systems, as well as small overt or covert self-contained video recording devices. In addition, it provides wireless audio surveillance receivers, wireless synthesized audio transmitters and repeaters, and digital voice recorders in a various covert disguise packages;

and wireless and wired video surveillance and monitoring systems, such as cameras, wireless transmitters, and receivers under the AidVision brand.

Further, the company offers under vehicle inspection systems to view the underside of vehicles entering and exiting controlled access points; under vehicle inspection screening software; and mobile specialty surveillance platforms.

Additionally, it distributes global positioning system vehicle tracking systems, thermal imaging, night vision equipment, pelican cases and lights, peerless handcuff and chains, defensive shields, roadside safety supply equipment, and CCTV cameras.

The company markets and distributes its products directly to federal, state, and local law enforcement agencies; state correctional facilities; the United States and allied military forces; private security personnel; and non-governmental agencies, and public and private companies.

The company was founded in 1972 and is based in Raleigh, North Carolina. On March 8, 2012, the voluntary petition of Law Enforcement Associates Corporation for reorganization under Chapter 11 was converted to Chapter 7. It had filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy on July 27, 2011." BusinessWeek

James Lindsay

George Washington University Age: 79

"James J. Lindsay serves as an Officer Candidate School Hall of Fame, US Army Science Board and Special Operations Advisory Group to the Secretary of Defense.

Gen. Lindsay enlisted in the Army in 1952, General Lindsay's command assignments range from platoon through MACOM in peacetime and war. His early assignments were with the 82nd Airborne Division and 77th Special Forces Group. He held eight different positions in the 82nd Airborne from platoon to division level. In Vietnam, he served with the Vietnamese Airborne Brigade, commanded the 2d Battalion 60th Infantry, 9th Infantry Division and was the G3, 9th Infantry Division. He also served as a General Officer, and commanded the 82nd Airborne Division, the Infantry School, and the XVIII Airborne Corps. He also served as Commander in Chief, United States Readiness Command and was the first Commander in Chief, United States Special Operations Command. General Lindsay organized and served as first commander of congressionally mandated U.S Special Operations Command, comprised of Special Operations Forces of all services: Navy Seals; Army Rangers and Green Berets; Air Force Air Commandos; and the counter-terrorist joint task force.

Unique responsibilities in the areas of: research, development, acquisition, procurement, programming and budgeting. Only one of ten Department of Defense unified commands with own separate (from Army, Navy, and Air Force) program and budget--$3.1 billion annually. General Lindsey trained and deployed over 500 teams to sixty-three different nations to include a joint special operations task force of over 4000 personnel with responsibility for most of the key targets on Operation Just Cause in Panama - December 1989.

General Lindsay retired on 1 July 1990. General Lindsay's awards and decorations include: Distinguished Service Cross, Distinguished Service Medal, Silver Star (with four Oak Leaf Clusters), Legion of Merit, and Bronze Star with 'V' device (with three Oak Leaf Clusters), Combat Infantryman's Badge, Master Parachutist Badge, Ranger Tab, and Special Forces Tab. He has been a Director of Law Enforcement Associates Corp. since August 23, 2004.

General Lindsay also serves as the president of the Airborne and Special Operations Museum Foundation, and a Senior Observer with the Army's Battle Command Training Program. General Lindsay has compiled an extraordinary career. Additionally, he is a member of the United States Army Ranger Hall of Fame. He was also the 1996 recipient of the Infantry's Doughboy Award, and the 1998 recipient of the United States Special Operations Command Bull Simon Award.

General Lindsay and his wife Gerry live in Vass, North Carolina. He graduated from the Army Officer Candidate School in May 1953 as a Second Lieutenant in the Infantry. His military education includes the Infantry Officer Advanced Course, the United States Marine Corps Command and Staff College and the National War College.

He holds a Bachelor's Degree from the University of Nebraska (Omaha) and a Master of Science Degree in Foreign Affairs from George Washington University." Businessweek
