Background

"Advanced Software Systems, Inc. provides information technology solutions to federal, state, and local governments, and Fortune 500 companies. It offers business alignment, managed, software engineering, acquisition planning, contract management, account management, program management, and business process outsourcing services.

The company serves independent software vendors; and insurance and finance, transportation and logistics, business and legal, banking and insurance, and non-profit companies." [Businessweek


"Advanced Software Systems Inc. (Assyst) is a global CMMI level 3 information technology solutions provider with a track record of successfully delivering end-to-end IT services to Federal, State and Local Governments and Fortune 500 companies. Our experienced functional and technical IT professionals are driven by a passion for technology, and share our Vision of being The One-Point Source (TOPS) in catering to the computing needs of our diverse clientele.

Assyst brings proven methodologies and processes, global expertise in application development, and a legacy of best practices and ready-to-fit frameworks to expedite the development cycle and reduce the total cost of ownership. Our ongoing investments in best practices, domain knowledge, technology, and infrastructure result in agile and rapid service delivery, providing our clients with the ability to quickly respond to the evolving needs of the marketplace. Our proven capabilities in designing and developing applications across varied technology platforms and business domains, as well as an efficient project management model ensures proper requirements capture and delivery of systems that fully meet those requirements." Assyst

"Federal

Assyst is a professional and technical services provider of Information Technology (IT), business applications and software engineering services for Civilian and DoD government clients. Assyst applies mature processes tailored for the Federal government market place to support acquisition planning, contract management, account management, and program management - the key success factors for performing multi-year, multi-team contracts. Assyst’s approach combines an Agile methodology for rapid deployment with the CMMi model to balance process and documentation requirements with service delivery.

Assyst continuously strives to improve performance by focusing on rapid service delivery, designing flexible solutions, and optimizing processes to improve quality and throughput. Assyst has established business processes and systems to support large task orders for Federal government clients. Assyst uses Earned Value Management, PMBOK, and principles established by the Project Management Institute (PMI) to manage the delivery of services. Assyst has mature processes that are repeatable, managed and optimized; we have Service Level Agreements (SLAs), and Operational Level Agreements (OLAs) that incorporate industry best practices and help reduce risk. Assyst has experience developing solutions in response to Performance Work Statements (PWS), working on projects and task orders on fixed-price terms based on milestone deliverables.

Assyst is a subject matter expert in implementing enterprise solutions that improve IT Governance, Portfolio Management, IV&V, Enterprise Architecture, Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP), Business Intelligence and Data Warehousing. Assyst offers extensive experience with commercial best practices, and we have a strong mission-support focus, and firmly established global alliances. Assyst is a leading provider of information technology, managed services and life-cycle solutions in the commercial sector and we know how to transfer commercial practices and technology to work in government agencies.

Assyst is involved in developing solutions for government that exploit newer technology areas such as SOA, Web Services, and Web 2.0. Assyst is developing templates to better define our SOA projects, developing services for better user interaction and using technology and techniques such as AJAX, DHTML, Flash or JAVA API with WSRP to better support more complex use cases, and better addresses interoperability.

Some of the newer technologies used by Assyst are based on common standards and include eXtensible Markup Language (XML), Simple Object Access Protocol (SOAP), Universal Description, Discovery and Integration (UDDI), Web Services Description Language (WSDL), and Representational State Transfer (REST).

Assyst has expertise in commercial and industry solutions that have potential applicability in the Federal sector. Areas of continuous investment include Social Networking, Collaboration, SOA, Web Services and Web 2.0. Areas of expertise include Refactoring, RSS, Mashups, Google Search, Flickr, BitTorrent, and Wikis. Assyst’s knowledge in AJAX, REST, and SharePoint, and our agile approach for rapid deployment allow us to attract and engage the best and brightest technologists. Our implementation team brings innovative technological solutions that enhance the workplace environment and improve business processes resulting in higher quality services." Assyst

"Dr. Stephen W. Ritterbush is a Partner at Fairfax Partners, LLC. He founded the firm in 1989. He is a Senior Partner at Fairfax Media Partners, LLC. Dr. Ritterbush is also a Managing Partner and Co-Founder at Fairfax Partners I, II, and III. He co-founded Fairfax Partners II in October 1996 to create and invest in growth companies.

Dr. Ritterbush also serves as an Outside Director at Gallagher Marine Systems.

He served as the Managing Director at USBX Advisory Services LLC.

Dr. Ritterbush also served as the President at Traxus Technologies, Inc.

In conjunction with Johnson & Johnson, Inc., he formed two separate co-investment partnerships: VanMed, Inc. and MedVest, Inc. Together with Johnson & Johnson, Dr. Ritterbush was a Co-Founder of three medical device companies and one drug company.

In addition, he was a Founder of APACHE Medical Systems, Inc.

From late 1989 to mid 1991, Dr. Ritterbush was the President and Chief Executive Officer at Greater Washington Investors, where he, along with the staff of Fairfax repositioned a stagnant, underperforming portfolio, ultimately resulting in an 11-fold increase in the company’s share price. There, he managed investments in more than 35 technology companies.

From 1986 to 1990, Dr. Ritterbush was a Founder, Senior Corporate Officer, and Director at ICF Kaiser International, Inc. and served as the President at American Venture Investments, Inc. From 1981 to 1986, he served as the President and Chief Executive Officer at Arthur D. Little Far East, Inc. in Singapore, where he worked primarily with the United States technology and venture capital companies seeking to establish operations in Asia.

While in the Far East and later in Washington, Dr. Ritterbush worked at Advent International of Boston to establish and develop venture capital funds in Singapore, Hong Kong, and Malaysia.

From 1979 to 1983, he worked at Arthur D. Little, Inc. in Cambridge as a Management Consultant. Dr. Ritterbush was responsible for developing and implementing corporate strategies to expand business in Asia.

He is currently the Chairman of HealthAspex. Dr. Ritterbush presently serves on the Board of Trustees of Union College and is the Chairman of the Finance Committee.

He was also awarded a Rockefeller Foundation Fellowship to the Belfer Center for Science and International Affairs, John F. Kennedy School of Government, Harvard University.

Dr. Ritterbush holds a Ph.D. and an M.A. from the Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy, Tufts University, an M.S. in Geophysics from the University of Hawaii, and Undergraduate degrees in Liberal Arts and Engineering from Union College."

Sunil Kumar, Founder,CEO

"Mr. Sunil Kumar founded Advanced Software Systems, Inc. in 1993 and serves as its Chief Executive Officer.

Mr. Kumar has 20 years experience in the Information Technology field. He is responsible for overseeing the key components of Advanced Software Systems growth including strategic planning, business development, selection and evaluation of the board members, supporting the operations and administration of the board and interfacing between the board and the staff and he is also responsible for recommending the yearly budgets and prudently managing the organization's resources and growth within the budget guidelines. Mr. Kumar also oversaw the implementation of a Advanced Software Systems-wide Employee Stock Option Plan, Employee Stock Ownership Plan and other benefits plans to motivate employee participation.

Mr. Kumar started his career as with Digital Equipment Corporation (DEC) supporting their custom development and MRP implementation on the VAX platform in India and Boston, Massachusetts. He was involved in pre-sales, design, development and implementation of a number of clients using VAX/VMS including Federal Home Loan Bank Board, Westinghouse and Kelloggs.

Mr. Kumar served in various capacities including Software Architect, Program Manager and Project Manager, leading enterprise wide initiatives for organizations such as IBM, Boeing (formerly, McDonald Douglas), Nortel Networks, Comsearch and International Monetary Fund.

Vice President of Monetization (The Wizard) at xAd [1], formerly V-Enable,"Sunil is responsible for the implementation of all our performance-driven campaigns. His group leads monetization, new partner integration, and advertising operations. Through his past experience in network architecture, Sunil participated in the rise of the storage over IP industry, working with industry leaders to bring quality technology to the market – a mission he now focuses on the mobile industry."

His experience ranges from managing and designing custom software solutions to implementing COTS ERP solutions.

He holds an MBA Marketing with an undergraduate in Physics, Mathematics and Statistics."


Shyama P. Mandal, President

"Mr. Shyama P. Mandal serves as President of Advanced Software Systems, Inc. (Assyst). Mr. Mandal is responsible for executing the strategies and vision of Advanced Software Systems. He joined Assyst in 1994.

In his career spanning over 20 years, he has held key positions in implementing large-scale information technology systems using leading-edge technologies.

Mr. Mandal started his career as a software engineer with CMC Ltd. At CMC, Mr. Mandal worked on implementing first nationwide data network based on IBM's Systems Network Architecture (SNA) in India. He also developed and implemented corporate MIS system for CMC. He later participated in re-engineering of mainframe based system used by London Underground for their time table and signal data generation. Mr. Mandal then led the design and implementation of the system for timetabling and operation plan generation system using UNIX and RDBMS for the world's largest and oldest metro railway.

Mr. Mandal worked as a consultant at IBM to automate the process of application of maintenance and upgrades to software portfolio in OS/390 environment using System Modification Program Extended (SMP/E).

Mr. Mandal has also worked at The World Bank as lead for financial system on VAX/VMS to disburse payment across the globe. He also consulted extensively at other international financial organizations to re-engineer, design, develop and deploy financial transaction systems.

Mr. Mandal holds an MS in Computer Engineering and a BS in Electronics & Electrical Communication Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur, India." Businessweek

Mr. Anderson served as Director of Operations of Advanced Software Systems, Inc. since 1997, and served as its Vice President of Operations in 1999. Mr. Anderson oversees aspects of Advanced Software Systems domestic and international operations, including corporate services, operations and sales and marketing services.

Mr. Anderson managed technicians, engineers and other managers, he set performance goals, and developed operational goals that were tied to long-term goals. Mr. Anderson monitored division performance against performance goals, and he ensured adherence to annual budget.

He holds B.S. (Industrial Engineering) from University of MTSU, and MBA from University of Virginia Tech."
